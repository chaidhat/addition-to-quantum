# Time Energy Uncertainty

Relies on [<span class="underline">quantum dynamics</span>](#quantum-dynamics).

Time is not an observable.

We could count $N$ full waves of frequency $\omega$ over a time window $T$. You can only count to within $\pm 1$ wave, so $\Delta N\geq 1$.

The uncertainty in the wavecount is $\Delta N$

We know that

$$N=\frac{\omega T}{2\pi} \quad\Rightarrow\quad \Delta N = \frac{\Delta \omega T}{2\pi} \geq 1 \quad \Rightarrow\quad \Delta \omega T \geq 2\pi$$

From [<span class="underline">(</span>](#planck-einstein-relation)Planck-Einstein relation),
$$\Delta E \cdot T\geq h = \frac{\hbar}{2}$$
